# Returns and Quality Complaints Policy

**Document ID:** DOC-011
**Owner:** Quality & Customer Care, Al Dente S.r.l.
**Applies to:** All B2B customers (retail chains, distributors, HoReCa, food service)
**Status:** In force

---

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This policy defines how Al Dente S.r.l. manages returns and quality complaints for its dry pasta products. It sets out the conditions under which a complaint qualifies, the evidence required, the defects covered, the cases that are excluded, and the outcomes the customer can expect.

The policy applies to all finished products supplied by Al Dente S.r.l. and is intended to ensure consistent, traceable, and fair handling of every claim.

---

## 2. Return Window

Complaints must be submitted within **15 days** from the delivery date.

Any complaint received after the 15-day return window has elapsed cannot be accepted, regardless of the nature of the defect reported.

---

## 3. Required Evidence

To open and process a complaint, the customer must provide **both** of the following:

- **Lot number** (as printed on the pack/carton)
- **Photo of the non-conformity**

A complaint missing either the lot number or the photo of non-conformity cannot be validated and will not proceed to a return outcome until the missing evidence is supplied within the return window.

---

## 4. Covered Defects

The following non-conformities are covered by this policy and, when evidenced and within the return window, qualify for a return outcome:

- **Bloated packs**
- **Broken pasta**
- **Foreign body**
- **Mislabeling**

---

## 5. Exclusions

The following cases are **not** covered by this policy and do not qualify for a return:

- **Storage damage at customer side**
- **Product past TMC** (minimum durability date / "termine minimo di conservazione")
- **Tampered packaging**

Complaints falling under any of the above exclusions will be rejected with the corresponding reason recorded.

---

## 6. Outcomes

When a complaint qualifies, the customer is offered one of the following remedies:

- **Replacement** of the affected product, or
- **Credit note on the lot value**

The applicable outcome is determined together with the customer during complaint handling, based on availability and customer preference.

---

## 7. Lot Blocking

When a quality complaint is confirmed, the affected lot is **blocked**.

Lot blocking prevents further shipment of units from the same lot pending internal investigation, and is a standing measure to contain any systemic non-conformity at the lot level.

---

## 8. Complaint Handling Summary

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Customer submits complaint within the 15-day return window |
| 2 | Customer provides lot number and photo of non-conformity |
| 3 | Quality & Customer Care verifies evidence against covered defects and exclusions |
| 4 | If confirmed, the affected lot is blocked |
| 5 | Outcome applied: replacement or credit note on the lot value |

> Note: a draft revision also discussed adding **off smell** to the covered defects, but it was not approved. The covered defect types remain the four listed in section 4.
