# Quality Management Policy

**Status:** Approved and in force

## 1. Purpose

This Quality Management Policy defines the principles, controls and responsibilities that govern product quality at Al Dente S.r.l. It establishes how quality is assured from the receipt of raw materials through to the release of finished dry pasta, ensuring that every batch placed on the market meets our internal standards and the expectations of our customers.

## 2. Scope

The scope of this policy covers **incoming raw materials and finished product QC**. It applies to:

- All raw materials received at the plant, primarily durum wheat semolina and process water, before they are accepted into production.
- All finished dry pasta products, at the point of quality control prior to release.

The two ends of the production chain - what enters the plant and what leaves it - are the two mandatory quality gates defined by this policy. Material that does not pass the incoming gate is not admitted to production; product that does not pass the finished-product gate is not released for sale.

## 3. Lot Traceability

**Lot traceability is maintained throughout the production process.** Every batch is assigned a unique lot identifier that links the finished product back to the raw materials, the production date and the relevant quality control records.

Lot traceability enables Al Dente S.r.l. to:

- Identify the origin and processing history of any unit placed on the market.
- Isolate and, where necessary, block a specific lot in the event of a confirmed non-conformity, without affecting unrelated production.
- Support customer complaints and returns handling, which are processed on a lot basis.

The lot number is the primary key that connects quality records, shipping documentation and customer-facing references.

## 4. Quality Control Checks

The following quality control checks are performed as part of finished-product QC:

| Check | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| **Moisture** | Verifies that the dried pasta has reached the correct residual moisture level, ensuring stability, shelf life and structural integrity. |
| **Color** | Confirms that the product presents the expected appearance and uniform coloring characteristic of durum wheat semolina pasta. |
| **Cooking test** | Assesses cooking behavior, hold and consistency of the pasta, validating that the product performs correctly in use. |

These three checks - **moisture**, **color** and **cooking test** - constitute the core finished-product control set. A batch must satisfy all three checks to be considered conforming.

## 5. Quality Gates

### 5.1 Incoming raw materials

Raw materials are inspected on receipt before being accepted into the production process. Materials that do not meet acceptance criteria are blocked and are not used in production.

### 5.2 Finished product

Finished dry pasta is subject to the quality control checks described in Section 4. Only batches that pass all checks and are correctly traceable to their lot are released. Batches that fail any check are held and, if the non-conformity is confirmed, the affected lot is blocked.

## 6. Responsibilities

- The **Quality Assurance Department** owns this policy, defines acceptance criteria and authorizes the release or blocking of lots.
- **Production** ensures that lot identification is applied and maintained throughout processing.
- **Goods-in personnel** perform incoming raw material checks and route material to acceptance or blocking accordingly.

## 7. Records

Quality control results for both incoming raw materials and finished product are recorded against the relevant lot. These records form the basis of traceability and are retained to support audits, customer enquiries and complaint resolution.
