# HACCP Plan Summary

**Document ID:** DOC-013
**Document type:** HACCP plan summary
**Company:** Al Dente S.r.l. - dry pasta manufacturer
**Scope:** Production of dry durum-wheat semolina pasta (500g retail formats)
**Status:** Approved and in force
**Owner:** Food Safety Team (HACCP Team Leader)
**Review cycle:** Annual, or upon any change to process, equipment, raw materials, or after a significant non-conformity

---

## 1. Purpose and scope

This document summarises the Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points (HACCP) plan
applied across Al Dente S.r.l. production. It defines the Critical Control Points (CCPs)
identified for the dry pasta process, together with the monitoring, corrective actions,
and verification activities applied at each one.

The plan covers the full process flow: incoming raw materials, semolina storage,
dough mixing, extrusion/forming, drying, cooling, foreign-body inspection, metal
detection, and packaging.

## 2. Plant statement

Al Dente S.r.l. operates an **egg-free plant**. No egg or egg-derived ingredients are
introduced anywhere in the facility. All products are made exclusively from durum wheat
semolina and water. This statement is a structural prerequisite of the plant and is
verified during supplier qualification, goods-in checks, and label control.

## 3. HACCP team and prerequisite programmes (PRPs)

The HACCP plan is built on a foundation of prerequisite programmes that keep the
operating environment under control, so that the CCPs can focus on the residual
significant hazards. Active PRPs include:

- Cleaning and sanitation of lines and contact surfaces
- Pest control and building integrity
- Personnel hygiene and training
- Maintenance and calibration of process and inspection equipment
- Supplier qualification and incoming raw-material control
- Allergen management and label control (supporting the egg-free plant statement)
- Lot traceability (one-up / one-down)

## 4. Critical Control Points (CCPs)

The following CCPs have been identified for the process. Each is monitored,
documented, and verified against defined critical limits.

| CCP | Control point | Hazard addressed |
| --- | --- | --- |
| CCP-1 | Drying temperature | Microbiological / product stability hazard from incorrect moisture reduction |
| CCP-2 | Foreign body control | Physical hazard from non-metallic foreign bodies |
| CCP-3 | Metal detection | Physical hazard from metal fragments |

The three CCPs of this plan are: **metal detection**, **drying temperature**, and
**foreign body control**.

### CCP-1 - Drying temperature

- **Hazard:** Insufficient or excessive drying compromising microbiological stability
  and shelf life of the dry pasta.
- **Monitoring:** Continuous monitoring of dryer temperature profile against the
  validated drying curve for each format. Final product moisture verified on a
  sampling basis.
- **Corrective action:** Adjust dryer parameters to the validated range; segregate and
  hold any product dried outside critical limits pending QC evaluation; block the
  affected lot if non-conformity is confirmed.
- **Records:** Dryer temperature logs and moisture-check records per lot.

### CCP-2 - Foreign body control

- **Hazard:** Presence of non-metallic foreign bodies (e.g. extraneous material) in the
  finished product.
- **Monitoring:** Inspection and screening of product flow prior to packaging; visual
  and screening controls at defined inspection stations.
- **Corrective action:** Reject and segregate affected product; investigate source;
  block the affected lot if a foreign-body non-conformity is confirmed.
- **Records:** Foreign-body inspection logs and any rejection records per lot.

### CCP-3 - Metal detection

- **Hazard:** Presence of metal fragments in the finished product.
- **Monitoring:** In-line metal detector on the packaging line; functional checks
  performed with certified test pieces at defined intervals and at line start-up.
- **Corrective action:** Automatic ejection of contaminated packs; hold and re-inspect
  product produced since the last successful detector check; block the affected lot if
  confirmed.
- **Records:** Metal-detector verification logs and ejection records per lot.

## 5. Monitoring, corrective actions and verification

For each CCP, monitoring is performed against documented critical limits. When a
deviation is detected, the defined corrective action is applied, the affected product
is controlled (held, segregated, or rejected), and, where a non-conformity is
confirmed, the affected lot is blocked.

Verification activities include review of monitoring records, calibration of
inspection and process equipment (notably the metal detector and dryer instrumentation),
internal audits, and periodic validation of the drying curves and detector performance.

## 6. Traceability and lot management

Full lot traceability is maintained one-up and one-down. Each finished lot can be
linked back to its raw-material lots and forward to dispatch. In the event of a
confirmed CCP deviation or quality complaint, the relevant lot is blocked and managed
according to the returns and quality complaints policy.

## 7. Records and document control

All CCP monitoring, corrective-action, and verification records are retained and
controlled as part of the food-safety management system. This summary is reviewed at
least annually and whenever a process, equipment, raw-material, or regulatory change
could affect the hazard analysis.
