# Incoming Goods QC Procedure

**Document ID:** DOC-016
**Issued by:** Al Dente S.r.l. - Quality Assurance Department
**Scope:** Inbound raw materials and packaging at the Al Dente S.r.l. plant
**Applies to:** Goods-in operators, QC technicians, Warehouse supervisor
**Related documents:** Quality management policy (DOC-012), HACCP plan, Returns and quality complaints policy (DOC-011)

---

## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines how every incoming delivery of raw materials (durum wheat semolina, water-related inputs) and packaging is checked, documented and either released into production stock or blocked, before it can be used by Al Dente S.r.l.

The objective is to ensure that only conforming materials enter the production flow, that any non-conformity is traced to the supplier lot, and that decisions are recorded for audit.

---

## 2. Procedure steps

The incoming goods quality control follows four mandatory steps, in order:

1. **Sampling**
2. **Moisture check**
3. **Documentation**
4. **Release or block**

No delivery may be moved to usable stock until all four steps have been completed and recorded.

---

## 3. Step detail

### 3.1 Sampling

On arrival, the QC technician draws representative samples from the delivery according to the sampling plan. Samples are taken from multiple points of the lot so that they are representative of the whole consignment. Each sample is labelled with the supplier name, delivery date, and supplier lot number, and linked to the goods-in record.

### 3.2 Moisture check

The sampled material is subjected to a **moisture check** against the acceptance limits defined in the Quality management policy (DOC-012). Semolina outside the moisture specification is a non-conformity. The measured moisture value is recorded for each lot and kept with the sampling record.

### 3.3 Documentation

The outcome of sampling and the moisture check is recorded in the incoming goods QC documentation. This includes: supplier, supplier lot number, delivery date, quantity, sample reference, measured moisture value, the pass/fail assessment, and the name of the operator. This documentation is the formal evidence supporting the release-or-block decision and is retained for traceability and audit.

### 3.4 Release or block

Based on the documented results:

- **Release** - if sampling and the moisture check are within specification, the lot is released into usable production stock and made available to manufacturing.
- **Block** - if any check is out of specification, the lot is blocked. Blocked material is segregated, clearly identified as not usable, and cannot be drawn for production. The block decision, with its reason, is recorded; disposition (return to supplier or rejection) is handled in line with the Quality management policy and the supplier agreement.

---

## 4. Records and traceability

Each incoming lot is traceable through its goods-in QC record from supplier lot number to the release-or-block decision. These records support downstream traceability of finished product lots and are made available during internal audits and customer or certification audits.

---

## 5. Responsibilities

- **Goods-in operator:** receives the delivery, checks delivery documents, calls QC before stock is made usable.
- **QC technician:** performs sampling and the moisture check, records results, proposes release or block.
- **Warehouse supervisor:** ensures blocked lots are segregated and that only released lots enter production stock.

---

This procedure is part of the Al Dente S.r.l. quality management system. Any deviation must be authorised by the Quality Assurance Department and recorded.
