# Returns Handling Procedure

**Document ID:** DOC-017
**Type:** Operating Procedure
**Owner:** Quality Assurance / Customer Service
**Company:** Al Dente S.r.l.
**Status:** Active

---

## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines the standard operating steps for handling customer returns
and quality complaints related to dry pasta products manufactured by Al Dente S.r.l.
It ensures that every complaint is processed consistently, that defective lots are
contained where necessary, and that the customer receives a timely and correct
resolution.

This procedure is the operational complement to the **Returns and quality complaints
policy (DOC-011)**. Where the policy defines *what* is covered, this procedure defines
*how* a return is processed end to end.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all returns and quality complaints received from GDO customers, distributors,
food-service customers, and HoReCa accounts for finished dry pasta products, regardless
of the channel through which the complaint is received (phone, email, customer portal).

## 3. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| Customer Service Agent | Opens the complaint, collects evidence, communicates with the customer |
| Quality Assurance (QA) | Verifies the non-conformity, decides on lot blocking, validates the resolution |
| Warehouse / Logistics | Executes replacements and handles physical returned goods |
| Finance / Administration | Issues credit notes when applicable |

## 4. Procedure Steps

The returns handling workflow consists of the following five steps, executed in order:

1. **open complaint**
2. **verify lot + photo**
3. **apply policy**
4. **block lot if confirmed**
5. **credit note or replacement**

Each step is detailed below.

### 4.1 Open complaint

When a customer reports a problem, the Customer Service Agent opens a new complaint
record. The agent captures:

- Customer name and account ID
- Product name and SKU
- Reported defect (e.g. bloated packs, broken pasta, foreign body, mislabeling)
- Date the issue was reported
- Quantity affected

A unique complaint reference is assigned and the case is set to status *Open*. The
complaint must be opened even if it is not yet clear whether it qualifies for a return,
so that no report is lost.

### 4.2 Verify lot + photo

Before any resolution is offered, the agent must verify the evidence required by the
returns policy. The complaint can only proceed when **both** of the following are present:

- the **lot number** printed on the affected packs, and
- a **photo of the non-conformity**.

The agent checks that the lot number is valid and traceable in the production system and
that the supplied photo clearly shows the reported defect. The agent also confirms that
the complaint falls within the **15-day return window** from delivery.

If the lot number is missing, the photo is missing, or the report is outside the return
window, the complaint does not qualify and is rejected with a clear reason recorded on
the case. Common rejection reasons include *missing photo evidence* and *outside 15-day
return window*.

### 4.3 Apply policy

With evidence verified, QA applies the **Returns and quality complaints policy
(DOC-011)** to determine eligibility:

- **Covered defects:** bloated packs, broken pasta, foreign body, mislabeling.
- **Exclusions:** storage damage at customer side, product past TMC (best-before),
  tampered packaging.

If the defect is covered and not excluded, the complaint is confirmed as eligible. If
the defect falls under an exclusion, the complaint is closed as not eligible with the
reason recorded.

### 4.4 Block lot if confirmed

When a non-conformity is **confirmed** (for example a foreign body or a mislabeling that
points to a process or labeling issue), QA places a **hold on the affected lot**. Lot
blocking leverages full lot traceability and serves to:

- prevent further shipment of potentially affected stock,
- trigger an internal investigation into root cause, and
- protect other customers who may have received the same lot.

The lot remains blocked until QA completes its assessment and either releases it or
disposes of the affected stock. The blocking decision and rationale are recorded on the
complaint.

### 4.5 Credit note or replacement

For eligible complaints, the resolution is one of the two outcomes allowed by policy:

- **replacement** of the affected goods, or
- **credit note** on the lot value.

The agreed outcome is communicated to the customer and executed by the responsible
function (Warehouse for replacements, Finance for credit notes). The complaint is then
updated to status *Resolved* with the resolution and any reference documents attached,
and closed.

## 5. Process Flow

```
open complaint
      |
      v
verify lot + photo  --(missing / out of window)--> reject, record reason, close
      |
      v
apply policy  --(excluded / not covered)--> close, not eligible
      |
      v
block lot if confirmed
      |
      v
credit note or replacement --> resolve and close
```

## 6. Records

The following records are kept for each complaint:

- Complaint reference and full case history
- Lot number and photo evidence
- Eligibility decision and applied policy reference
- Lot blocking decision (where applicable)
- Final resolution (credit note or replacement)

Records are retained in line with the company quality management system to support
traceability and audits.
