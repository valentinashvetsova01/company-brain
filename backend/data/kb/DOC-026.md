# Allergen management policy

**Document ID:** DOC-026
**Issued by:** Al Dente S.r.l. - Quality Assurance Department
**Scope:** Allergen identification, control and communication across raw materials and finished pasta produced at the Al Dente S.r.l. plant
**Applies to:** QC technicians, Production operators, Warehouse supervisor, Procurement
**Related documents:** Quality management policy (DOC-012), HACCP plan (DOC-013), Incoming goods QC procedure (DOC-016), Returns and quality complaints policy (DOC-011)

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines how Al Dente S.r.l. identifies, controls and communicates food allergens across its raw materials and finished pasta. It ensures that the allergens present in our products are correctly declared, that the risk of unintended cross-contact is minimised, and that customers and consumers receive accurate allergen information.

The policy supports the HACCP plan (DOC-013), which treats allergen cross-contact as a recognised food safety hazard, and the Quality management policy (DOC-012).

---

## 2. Allergens present at the plant

Al Dente S.r.l. produces durum wheat semolina pasta. The following allergen status applies to products manufactured at the plant.

| Category | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Declared plant allergen | Gluten (from durum wheat semolina) |
| Possible traces | Soy, mustard |
| Egg | The plant is egg-free; no egg or egg-derived ingredient is used in any product |

**Gluten** is an intrinsic constituent of our durum wheat semolina and is always declared on product labelling and specifications. Because shared logistics and shared upstream supply can introduce low-level cross-contact, our products may carry **possible traces of soy and mustard**, and this is communicated through precautionary allergen labelling where applicable.

The plant is **egg-free**: no egg or egg-derived ingredient enters the site or the production flow. This status is verified through supplier documentation and incoming controls.

---

## 3. Allergen controls

Three mandatory controls govern allergen management at Al Dente S.r.l. Each is operated and recorded as part of the quality management system.

### 3.1 Supplier allergen declarations

Every raw material supplier must provide an up-to-date **allergen declaration** stating the allergens present in, and the cross-contact risks associated with, the material supplied. These declarations are reviewed by Procurement and Quality Assurance before a material is approved and are checked against incoming deliveries under the incoming goods QC procedure (DOC-016). A material cannot enter usable stock without a valid allergen declaration on file. The declarations are the documentary basis for confirming the egg-free status of the plant and for identifying the soy and mustard cross-contact risk.

### 3.2 Dedicated raw material storage

Raw materials are held in **dedicated storage** that separates materials by allergen profile and prevents cross-contact during handling and warehousing. Storage locations are clearly identified, and the warehouse supervisor ensures that materials are not co-mingled in a way that could transfer allergens. This control reduces the risk of an unintended allergen reaching the production line through the raw material flow.

### 3.3 Line cleaning between format changes

Production lines are subject to documented **cleaning between format changes**. Cleaning removes residual material from the previous run before a new format is started, limiting carry-over and cross-contact between production runs. The cleaning is recorded so that each run can be linked to the cleaning performed before it, supporting both allergen control and lot traceability.

---

## 4. Communication and labelling

The allergen information for each product reflects the controls above:

- **Gluten** is declared as an ingredient-derived allergen on all relevant labelling and specifications.
- **Soy and mustard** are communicated as possible traces through precautionary labelling where applicable.
- The **egg-free** status is stated where customers require confirmation.

Allergen information shared with retail and trade customers forms part of product specifications and supporting documentation. Where a customer raises an allergen-related complaint, it is handled under the returns and quality complaints policy (DOC-011).

---

## 5. Responsibilities

- **Procurement:** obtains and maintains supplier allergen declarations; ensures no egg-derived material is sourced.
- **QC technician:** verifies allergen declarations against incoming deliveries under DOC-016 and confirms allergen status of lots.
- **Warehouse supervisor:** maintains dedicated raw material storage and prevents co-mingling of materials.
- **Production operators:** perform and record line cleaning between format changes.
- **Quality Assurance Department:** owns this policy, approves allergen declarations, and authorises any deviation.

---

This policy is part of the Al Dente S.r.l. quality management system and is operated in line with the HACCP plan (DOC-013). Any deviation must be authorised by the Quality Assurance Department and recorded.
