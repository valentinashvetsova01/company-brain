# Supplier Quality Agreement - Semolina

**Document ID:** DOC-027
**Issued by:** Al Dente S.r.l. - Quality Assurance Department
**Scope:** Quality requirements governing the supply of durum wheat semolina to the Al Dente S.r.l. plant
**Applies to:** Semolina suppliers, Procurement, QC technicians, Quality Assurance Department
**Related documents:** Quality management policy (DOC-012), HACCP plan (DOC-013), GDO capitolato (DOC-014), Incoming goods QC procedure (DOC-016), Returns and quality complaints policy (DOC-011), Returns handling procedure (DOC-017)

---

## 1. Purpose and scope

This agreement defines the quality requirements that apply to semolina suppliers delivering durum wheat semolina to Al Dente S.r.l. It sets out the documentation, acceptance specifications and audit obligations that each supplier must meet for their material to be accepted into the production flow.

The objective is to ensure that every consignment of semolina is conforming, fully documented and traceable to the supplier lot, so that only compliant material enters manufacturing. This agreement complements the incoming goods QC procedure (DOC-016), under which each delivery is sampled, checked and either released or blocked.

---

## 2. Applicability

This agreement **applies to semolina suppliers** and to all deliveries of durum wheat semolina destined for the Al Dente S.r.l. plant. By accepting a purchase order, the supplier accepts the requirements set out below for the supplied material.

Where this agreement refers to acceptance, blocking or disposition of non-conforming material, those activities are carried out in line with the incoming goods QC procedure (DOC-016) and the quality management policy (DOC-012).

---

## 3. Supplier requirements

Suppliers of semolina must comply with all of the following requirements:

| # | Requirement | Detail |
|---|-------------|--------|
| 1 | Certificate of analysis | A **certificate of analysis per delivery** must accompany every consignment, identifying the supplier lot and the measured parameters. |
| 2 | Moisture | Moisture content must be **max 14.5%**. |
| 3 | Protein | Protein content must be **min 12.5%**. |
| 4 | Mycotoxins | Mycotoxin content must respect the **mycotoxin limits per EU regulation**. |

### 3.1 Certificate of analysis per delivery

Each delivery must be supported by a certificate of analysis covering that specific consignment. The certificate must reference the supplier lot number so that it can be linked to the goods-in record created under DOC-016. Deliveries arriving without a valid certificate of analysis cannot be released into usable production stock.

### 3.2 Moisture - max 14.5%

The semolina supplied must have a moisture content of no more than 14.5%. This value is verified at incoming goods control through the moisture check defined in DOC-016. Material above the moisture limit is treated as a non-conformity.

### 3.3 Protein - min 12.5%

The semolina must have a protein content of at least 12.5%. This specification supports consistent dough behaviour and finished product quality, including the requirements set out in the GDO capitolato (DOC-014).

### 3.4 Mycotoxins - per EU regulation

Mycotoxin content must remain within the limits set by the applicable EU regulation. The supplier is responsible for ensuring that the durum wheat used for the semolina is sourced and handled so that these regulatory limits are met, and for evidencing compliance on the certificate of analysis.

---

## 4. Acceptance, blocking and non-conformity

Conformity to the specifications in section 3 is verified at incoming goods control. Where a consignment meets the certificate, moisture, protein and mycotoxin requirements, the lot may be released into usable production stock. Where any requirement is not met, the lot is blocked, segregated and handled as a non-conformity under DOC-016.

Disposition of blocked or returned material, including complaints raised against the supplier, follows the returns and quality complaints policy (DOC-011) and the returns handling procedure (DOC-017). Repeated non-conformities may be escalated by the Quality Assurance Department and reviewed at the supplier audit.

---

## 5. Audits

Al Dente S.r.l. carries out a supplier quality audit at an **annual** frequency. The annual audit reviews the supplier's compliance with this agreement, including the consistency of certificates of analysis, conformity to the moisture, protein and mycotoxin specifications, and the supplier's own controls supporting those parameters.

The audit is aligned with the HACCP plan (DOC-013) and the quality management policy (DOC-012). Findings are recorded and shared with the supplier, and corrective actions are tracked to closure by the Quality Assurance Department.

---

## 6. Records and traceability

The primary semolina supplier under this agreement is Molino San Giorgio (supplier SUP-001). Some legacy goods-in notes spell it "Mulino San Giorgio"; this refers to the same supplier, Molino San Giorgio.


Certificates of analysis and incoming QC records are retained so that every accepted lot of semolina is traceable from the supplier lot number through to release or block. These records support downstream traceability of finished product lots and are made available during internal audits and customer or certification audits.

---

This agreement is part of the Al Dente S.r.l. quality management system. Any deviation must be authorised by the Quality Assurance Department and recorded.
