# Traceability and Mock Recall Procedure

**Document ID:** DOC-028
**Issued by:** Al Dente S.r.l. - Quality Assurance Department
**Scope:** Lot traceability and product recall readiness across the Al Dente S.r.l. supply chain, from incoming raw material lots to customer shipments
**Applies to:** QC technicians, Warehouse supervisor, Logistics coordinator, Customer service, Quality Assurance Department
**Related documents:** Quality management policy (DOC-012), HACCP plan (DOC-013), Incoming goods QC procedure (DOC-016), Returns and quality complaints policy (DOC-011), Returns handling procedure (DOC-017), GDO supply specification (DOC-014)

---

## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines how Al Dente S.r.l. traces a product lot through the full supply chain and how it conducts a mock recall to verify that the traceability system works end to end before a real incident occurs.

The objective is twofold: to guarantee that any finished-product lot can be linked backwards to the raw material lots it was made from and forwards to the customers it was shipped to, and to demonstrate, through periodic rehearsal, that the company can locate and isolate affected product quickly. Effective traceability supports the HACCP plan (DOC-013) and the quality management policy (DOC-012), and underpins the obligations agreed with large-scale retail customers under the GDO supply specification (DOC-014).

---

## 2. Procedure steps

A traceability exercise, whether triggered by a real concern or run as a mock recall, follows five mandatory steps, in order:

1. **Identify lot**
2. **Trace raw material lots upstream**
3. **Trace shipments downstream**
4. **Notify affected customers**
5. **Reconcile quantities**

No traceability exercise is considered complete until all five steps have been carried out and recorded.

---

## 3. Step detail

### 3.1 Identify lot

The exercise begins by identifying the finished-product lot under investigation. The lot is the unit of traceability: every subsequent step is anchored to it. The QC technician records what is known about the lot, including the product, the production date, and the reason the lot has been selected (a quality concern, a customer complaint, or a scheduled mock recall).

### 3.2 Trace raw material lots upstream

From the identified finished-product lot, the team traces backwards to every raw material lot consumed in its production. This relies on the incoming goods QC records described in the incoming goods QC procedure (DOC-016), where each raw material lot is recorded with its supplier lot number and release decision. Upstream tracing confirms which incoming lots fed the finished product and whether any of them were associated with a prior non-conformity.

### 3.3 Trace shipments downstream

The team then traces forwards from the finished-product lot to every shipment that contained it. This step establishes which customers received product from the lot, in what quantities, and on which delivery dates. Downstream tracing is what makes targeted action possible: it limits the scope of any recall to the customers actually affected, rather than the whole customer base.

### 3.4 Notify affected customers

Once the affected customers are known, customer service notifies them. Notification is limited to the customers identified in the downstream trace. Where the affected product was supplied under the GDO supply specification (DOC-014), notification follows the communication terms agreed with that customer. Any subsequent returns are handled in line with the returns and quality complaints policy (DOC-011) and the returns handling procedure (DOC-017).

### 3.5 Reconcile quantities

Finally, the team reconciles quantities. The quantity produced for the lot is balanced against the quantity shipped, the quantity still in stock, and, in a real recall, the quantity recovered or accounted for at customers. Reconciliation confirms that no part of the lot is unaccounted for. The target for completing reconciliation, and therefore the full mock-recall exercise, is **4 hours** from the moment the lot is identified.

---

## 4. Mock recall frequency

Al Dente S.r.l. runs a mock recall **twice per year**. Each exercise selects a real finished-product lot and runs it through all five steps as if a genuine recall were under way, up to but not including actual customer recovery. The exercise is timed against the 4-hour reconciliation target, and any gap that prevents the target from being met is logged as a corrective action and reviewed by the Quality Assurance Department.

---

## 5. Records and traceability

Every traceability exercise produces a record linking the identified lot, the upstream raw material lots, the downstream shipments and customers, the notifications issued, and the final quantity reconciliation. These records are retained for audit and made available during internal audits and customer or certification audits, alongside the incoming goods QC records (DOC-016) on which upstream tracing depends.

---

## 6. Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| QC technician | Identifies the lot, performs upstream tracing, records measured results |
| Logistics coordinator | Performs downstream tracing of shipments and customers |
| Customer service | Notifies the affected customers identified in the downstream trace |
| Warehouse supervisor | Confirms in-stock quantities for reconciliation, segregates affected lots |
| Quality Assurance Department | Owns the procedure, schedules the twice-per-year mock recall, reviews timing against the 4-hour target |

---

This procedure is part of the Al Dente S.r.l. quality management system. Any deviation must be authorised by the Quality Assurance Department and recorded.
