# Pest Control Program Summary

**Document ID:** DOC-029
**Issued by:** Al Dente S.r.l. - Quality Assurance Department
**Scope:** Pest prevention, monitoring and control across the Al Dente S.r.l. plant, including production, storage and goods-in areas
**Applies to:** Quality Assurance Department, Warehouse supervisor, Production staff, the external certified provider
**Related documents:** HACCP plan (DOC-013), Quality management policy (DOC-012), Incoming Goods QC Procedure (DOC-016)

---

## 1. Purpose

This procedure summarises the pest control program in place at the Al Dente S.r.l. plant. The objective is to keep the manufacturing environment free of pests that could compromise food safety, contaminate raw materials such as durum wheat semolina, or damage packaging and finished product.

Pest control is a prerequisite program supporting the HACCP plan (DOC-013). It works alongside incoming material controls (DOC-016) to ensure that the plant environment, and not only individual lots, remains under control and fit for food production.

---

## 2. Program management

The pest control program is delivered by an **external certified provider** under a service agreement managed by the Quality Assurance Department. Using an external certified provider ensures that interventions, products used, and monitoring devices are handled by qualified personnel in line with applicable regulations.

The provider operates on a fixed schedule and reports to the Quality Assurance Department after each intervention. The Warehouse supervisor coordinates site access and ensures that monitoring devices remain in place and undisturbed.

---

## 3. Visit frequency

The external certified provider performs scheduled site visits on a **monthly** basis. Each monthly visit covers inspection of the plant, servicing of monitoring devices, assessment of any pest activity, and recording of findings.

Additional visits may be requested by the Quality Assurance Department outside the monthly cycle if monitoring or staff observation indicates a potential pest issue that requires a faster response.

---

## 4. Monitoring devices

The plant is protected by two complementary types of monitoring device, deployed and serviced by the external certified provider:

| Device | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| Bait stations | Detection and control of rodents along perimeters and risk points |
| Insect light traps | Detection and capture of flying insects in production and storage areas |

Both **bait stations** and **insect light traps** are positioned according to a site map agreed with the provider. At each monthly visit the devices are inspected, captured pests are assessed, consumables are replaced where needed, and any trend in activity is noted. Devices must not be moved or switched off by plant staff; any device found damaged or displaced is reported to the Quality Assurance Department.

---

## 5. Documentation

The pest control program is documented in a dedicated **register kept at the plant entrance**. The register collects, for each monthly visit, the date of intervention, the areas covered, the condition and readings of the bait stations and insect light traps, any pest activity observed, the actions taken by the external certified provider, and the name of the technician.

Keeping the register at the plant entrance makes the pest control history immediately available to the Quality Assurance Department, to the external certified provider on arrival, and to auditors. This documentation is the formal evidence that the program is active and effective, and is retained for traceability and audit, consistent with the records discipline applied to incoming material controls (DOC-016).

---

## 6. Responsibilities

- **External certified provider:** carries out the monthly visits, services the bait stations and insect light traps, applies control measures, and records each intervention in the register.
- **Quality Assurance Department:** manages the service agreement, reviews the register, requests additional visits when needed, and integrates pest control into the HACCP plan (DOC-013).
- **Warehouse supervisor:** coordinates site access, protects monitoring devices in storage and goods-in areas, and reports any pest sighting or damaged device.
- **Production staff:** maintain hygiene and housekeeping that limit pest harbourage, and report any sign of pest activity without delay.

---

This procedure is part of the Al Dente S.r.l. quality management system and supports the HACCP plan (DOC-013). Any deviation must be authorised by the Quality Assurance Department and recorded.
