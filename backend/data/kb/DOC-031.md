# Packaging specification - 500g carton box

**Document ID:** DOC-031
**Issued by:** Al Dente S.r.l. - Quality Assurance Department
**Scope:** Secondary packaging for the 500g retail format produced at the Al Dente S.r.l. plant
**Applies to:** Packaging operators, QC technicians, Warehouse supervisor, Procurement
**Related documents:** Quality management policy (DOC-012), HACCP plan (DOC-013), Incoming goods QC procedure (DOC-016), GDO capitolato (DOC-014)

---

## 1. Purpose

This specification defines the construction, materials and handling requirements for the carton box used for the 500g retail pasta format produced by Al Dente S.r.l. It sets the binding parameters that the carton must meet so that it protects the product, complies with food-contact requirements, and palletises consistently for distribution.

The objective is to ensure that every carton entering the plant and every carton placed on the market is conforming, traceable to its supplier lot, and compatible with the logistics units agreed with our customers.

---

## 2. Material and construction

The 500g carton box is manufactured from **recycled cardboard with a food-contact barrier**. The barrier layer is what allows the carton to be in direct or indirect contact with the food product without compromising safety, while the recycled cardboard base supports the sustainability targets of the company.

Key construction parameters:

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Material | Recycled cardboard with food-contact barrier |
| Empty box weight | 28 g |
| Print | Food-grade ink |

The empty box weight of **28 g** is a controlled parameter: it is used both for tare calculation on the filling lines and as an incoming acceptance attribute. Cartons deviating materially from 28 g are treated as a non-conformity.

---

## 3. Printing

All graphics, mandatory labelling and lot coding are applied using **food-grade ink**. The use of food-grade ink is mandatory for this format because the printed surface and the food-contact barrier together must not introduce any migration risk to the product.

Print quality (legibility of mandatory information, lot code and best-before date) is verified on the line. Illegible or smeared printing is a non-conformity and the affected cartons are segregated.

---

## 4. Logistics configuration

The 500g carton is grouped into standard distribution units as follows:

| Unit | Quantity |
|---|---|
| Cartons per case | 20 units |
| Case layers per pallet | 8 layers |

Each shipping case contains **20 carton units** of the 500g format. Cases are stacked on the pallet in **8 layers**. This configuration is the reference logistics build for the format and must match the unit-load data declared to customers and the GDO capitolato (DOC-014). Any change to cartons per case or pallet layers requires re-approval, because it affects pallet stability, transport planning and the data shared with distribution partners.

---

## 5. Incoming control and quality

Incoming deliveries of the 500g carton are checked under the Incoming goods QC procedure (DOC-016): sampling, verification against this specification (material, empty box weight of 28 g, food-grade ink print quality), documentation and a release-or-block decision. Cartons that do not meet this specification are blocked and segregated and cannot be drawn for production.

Acceptance limits and the handling of non-conforming packaging follow the Quality management policy (DOC-012). Food-safety aspects of the packaging, including the food-contact barrier and food-grade ink, are managed within the HACCP plan (DOC-013).

---

## 6. Responsibilities

- **Procurement:** sources cartons that meet this specification and ensures suppliers are aware of the binding parameters.
- **QC technician:** verifies incoming cartons against material, weight and print requirements; records results; proposes release or block.
- **Packaging operator:** uses only released cartons, monitors print legibility on the line, builds cases of 20 units.
- **Warehouse supervisor:** ensures pallets are built to 8 layers and that only conforming cartons reach the filling lines.

---

This specification is part of the Al Dente S.r.l. quality management system. Any deviation must be authorised by the Quality Assurance Department and recorded.
