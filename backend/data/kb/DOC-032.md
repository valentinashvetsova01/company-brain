# Cleaning and Sanitation Plan Summary

**Document ID:** DOC-032
**Issued by:** Al Dente S.r.l. - Quality Assurance Department
**Scope:** Routine cleaning and sanitation of production lines and storage silos at the Al Dente S.r.l. plant
**Applies to:** Production operators, Sanitation team, QC technicians, Warehouse supervisor
**Related documents:** Quality management policy (DOC-012), HACCP plan (DOC-013), Incoming goods QC procedure (DOC-016)

---

## 1. Purpose

This procedure summarises how production lines and raw-material silos at the Al Dente S.r.l. plant are cleaned and sanitised, and how the result of each cleaning activity is verified before the equipment is returned to use.

The objective is to keep the production environment under hygienic control, to prevent cross-contamination between different product formats, and to ensure that every cleaning activity is documented and verified in a way that supports the plant HACCP plan (DOC-013) and the quality management policy (DOC-012).

---

## 2. Cleaning regimes

The plan defines two distinct cleaning regimes, one for the production lines and one for the storage silos. Each regime has its own trigger and method, summarised below.

| Asset | Method | Trigger / frequency |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Production lines | Dry cleaning | After each format change |
| Storage silos | Deep cleaning | Quarterly |

The two regimes are managed independently: a line cleaning is driven by the production schedule, while a silo cleaning is driven by the calendar.

---

## 3. Regime detail

### 3.1 Production lines

The production lines are subjected to **dry cleaning after each format change**. Because the lines handle dry semolina and dry pasta, water is not introduced into the line during routine cleaning; the equipment is cleared of residual product using dry methods so that material from the previous format cannot carry over into the next production run.

A format change is the controlling event: whenever production switches from one pasta format to another, the affected line must be dry cleaned before the new format is started. This makes the cleaning frequency proportional to the actual number of changeovers rather than a fixed clock, and it directly addresses the cross-contamination hazard between formats identified in the HACCP plan (DOC-013).

### 3.2 Storage silos

The raw-material storage silos are subjected to **quarterly deep cleaning**. Deep cleaning is a scheduled, more thorough intervention than the routine line cleaning: the silo is emptied and cleaned internally so that no residual material accumulates over time. The quarterly cadence is fixed on the calendar and is planned around incoming-goods activity so that silos can be taken out of service in turn without interrupting the supply of conforming semolina released under the incoming goods QC procedure (DOC-016).

---

## 4. Verification

No cleaned asset is returned to use on the strength of the cleaning activity alone. Each cleaning is verified, and the verification combines two complementary checks:

- **Visual inspection** - the cleaned surfaces and accessible internal parts are inspected by eye to confirm that no visible residue, build-up, or foreign material remains.
- **ATP swab** - an ATP swab is taken from defined points of the cleaned asset to confirm hygienic effectiveness beyond what the eye can detect. The ATP result provides an objective measure that the surface is clean and not merely visibly tidy.

The combination of **visual inspection + ATP swab** is the standard verification for both regimes. The outcome of each verification is recorded against the asset and the cleaning event, and an unsatisfactory result requires the cleaning to be repeated and re-verified before the line or silo can be released back into production.

---

## 5. Records and traceability

Each cleaning activity is recorded with the asset identity, the regime applied (line dry cleaning after a format change, or quarterly silo deep cleaning), the date, the operator, and the verification result (visual inspection and ATP swab). These records form the evidence that the sanitation plan is being executed as defined, and they are made available during internal audits and customer or certification audits in line with the quality management policy (DOC-012).

---

## 6. Responsibilities

- **Production operator:** signals format changes and ensures the affected line is not restarted until dry cleaning and verification are complete.
- **Sanitation team:** performs the line dry cleaning and the quarterly silo deep cleaning according to this plan.
- **QC technician:** performs or oversees the verification (visual inspection and ATP swab) and records the result.
- **Warehouse supervisor:** schedules silo deep cleaning so that only properly cleaned silos hold raw material.

---

This procedure is part of the Al Dente S.r.l. quality management system. Any deviation must be authorised by the Quality Assurance Department and recorded.
