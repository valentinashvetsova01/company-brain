# Metal Detection CCP Monitoring

**Document ID:** DOC-033
**Issued by:** Al Dente S.r.l. - Quality Assurance Department
**Scope:** Metal detection critical control point on finished pasta packaging lines at the Al Dente S.r.l. plant
**Applies to:** Line operators, QC technicians, Quality Assurance Department
**Related documents:** HACCP plan (DOC-013), Quality management policy (DOC-012), Incoming Goods QC Procedure (DOC-016), GDO capitolato (DOC-014)

---

## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines how the metal detection critical control point (CCP) is monitored on the finished-product packaging lines at Al Dente S.r.l. Metal detection is a CCP within the HACCP plan (DOC-013): it is the control step that prevents metal contamination from reaching the consumer.

The objective is to ensure that every lot passing through the line is protected by a verified, functioning metal detector, that any loss of control is detected promptly, and that the affected product is contained and reassessed before it can be released.

---

## 2. Monitoring frequency

The metal detector is functionally verified by a test routine performed **every 2 hours** during production on each active line. A test is also performed at start-up, after any changeover, and at the end of the production run.

Each verification is logged with the time, the line, the result, and the name of the operator who performed it. The 2-hour interval is the maximum allowed gap between two consecutive valid tests; if the interval is exceeded, the period since the last good test is treated as not verified.

---

## 3. Test pieces

Each verification uses three certified test pieces, passed through the detector in the normal product flow:

| Test piece | Nominal size |
|------------|--------------|
| Ferrous (Fe) | 1.5 mm |
| Non-ferrous (non-Fe) | 2.0 mm |
| Stainless steel (SS) | 2.5 mm |

A test is valid only if all three pieces - **Fe 1.5 mm**, **non-Fe 2.0 mm** and **SS 2.5 mm** - are correctly detected and rejected by the automatic reject system. If any one of the three is not detected or not rejected, the test fails and the CCP is considered out of control.

---

## 4. Action on a failed test

When a verification fails, or when the detector or reject system is found not to be working correctly, the operator follows the deviation action without delay:

1. **Stop the line** - production on the affected line is halted immediately.
2. **Isolate the lot** - the product associated with the line is segregated, clearly identified as on hold, and held physically separate from released stock so it cannot be drawn for dispatch.
3. **Recheck since the last good test** - all product packaged since the last valid (good) test is reprocessed through a verified detector or otherwise reassessed, because that material was produced in a period that can no longer be considered verified.

The full action is: **stop line, isolate lot, recheck since last good test.** The line may resume only after the detector has been confirmed functional with a fresh passing test on all three pieces. The deviation, its cause, the quantity of affected product, and the disposition are recorded and reviewed by the Quality Assurance Department.

---

## 5. Records and traceability

Every metal detection verification, whether passing or failing, is recorded in the CCP monitoring log for the line. Each finished-product lot is traceable to the verifications that bracket its production window, so that for any lot it is possible to show the last good test before it and the first good test after it.

These records are part of the HACCP plan evidence (DOC-013), support traceability requirements toward GDO customers under the capitolato (DOC-014), and are made available during internal audits and customer or certification audits. Any product reassessed following a deviation is linked to its original lot and to the corrective action record.

---

## 6. Responsibilities

- **Line operator:** performs the 2-hourly verification with the three test pieces, logs the result, and on a failed test executes stop line, isolate lot, recheck since last good test.
- **QC technician:** confirms the detector is functional before the line resumes, oversees the recheck of affected product, and validates the corrective action.
- **Quality Assurance Department:** reviews deviations, authorises any release of reassessed product, and ensures the CCP monitoring records are complete.

---

This procedure is part of the Al Dente S.r.l. quality management system and of the HACCP plan. Any deviation from this procedure must be authorised by the Quality Assurance Department and recorded.
