# Sustainability summary 2025

**Document ID:** DOC-034
**Issued by:** Al Dente S.r.l. - Quality Assurance Department
**Scope:** Plant operations only
**Applies to:** Plant management, Operations staff, Quality assurance team
**Related documents:** Quality management policy (DOC-012), HACCP plan (DOC-013), Incoming goods QC procedure (DOC-016), GDO supply specification (DOC-014)

---

## 1. Purpose

This document summarises the sustainability performance of Al Dente S.r.l. for the year 2025. It provides a consolidated view of the environmental indicators that the company tracks and reports, and sets out the direction of improvement for the coming period.

The summary is intended as a reference for plant management, operations staff and the quality assurance team, and as supporting evidence during internal reviews and customer or certification audits. All figures reported here relate to plant operations only; activities outside the plant boundary (such as inbound logistics performed by third parties and downstream distribution) are not included in this summary.

---

## 2. Scope and boundary

The boundary of this summary is plant operations only. This means the indicators below cover the energy, materials and water used directly in manufacturing and packaging activities at the Al Dente S.r.l. plant. Choosing a clear and consistent boundary ensures that year-on-year comparisons are meaningful and that the data can be verified against plant records.

Where sustainability touches other parts of the management system, the relevant procedures continue to apply. Incoming materials and packaging are checked under the incoming goods QC procedure (DOC-016), product quality is governed by the quality management policy (DOC-012), and food safety controls follow the HACCP plan (DOC-013).

---

## 3. Key indicators 2025

The table below presents the headline sustainability indicators for plant operations in 2025.

| Indicator | 2025 value | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Renewable electricity | 60% | Share of electricity consumed by plant operations sourced from renewable supply |
| Recycled cardboard | 85% | Share of cardboard used in packaging that is recycled content |
| Water reduction target | 10% | Target reduction in water use per unit of output for the next reporting period |

### 3.1 Renewable electricity

In 2025, 60% of the electricity consumed by plant operations was sourced from renewable supply. This share reflects the company's progressive shift toward lower-carbon energy and is one of the main levers for reducing the environmental footprint of manufacturing.

### 3.2 Recycled cardboard

Of the cardboard used for packaging at the plant, 85% is recycled content. Packaging materials remain subject to the same incoming controls as other inputs, so recycled-content cardboard is verified on receipt through the incoming goods QC procedure (DOC-016) before it enters usable stock.

### 3.3 Water reduction target

For the next reporting period the company has set a water reduction target of 10%, measured against water use within plant operations. Water-related inputs and their management remain aligned with the requirements of the quality management policy (DOC-012) and the HACCP plan (DOC-013).

---

## 4. Approach and governance

Sustainability indicators are collected from plant records and reviewed by the quality assurance team alongside other operational data. Keeping sustainability data within the same management system as quality and food safety avoids duplication and ensures consistency: the same lots, materials and processes are described once and used for multiple purposes.

The recycled-cardboard figure depends on the materials accepted under the incoming goods QC procedure (DOC-016), and the supply commitments made to large-scale retail customers are framed by the GDO supply specification (DOC-014). This integrated approach means that environmental targets are not treated in isolation but are embedded in the way the plant already operates.

---

## 5. Responsibilities

- **Plant management:** sets sustainability targets, allocates resources for improvement actions, and reviews progress.
- **Operations staff:** implement day-to-day practices that affect energy, materials and water use within plant operations.
- **Quality assurance team:** collects and verifies the indicators, maintains the records, and reports performance during internal and external audits.

---

This summary is part of the Al Dente S.r.l. management system. The reported indicators cover plant operations only and are reviewed and updated each reporting period by the Quality Assurance Department.
