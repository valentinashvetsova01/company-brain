# Export compliance note - EU labeling

**Document ID:** DOC-035
**Issued by:** Al Dente S.r.l. - Quality Assurance Department
**Scope:** Labeling of Al Dente S.r.l. finished pasta products destined for sale within the European Union
**Applies to:** Export sales, Packaging design, Quality Assurance, Production planning
**Related documents:** Quality management policy (DOC-012), HACCP plan (DOC-013), GDO capitolato (DOC-014), Returns and quality complaints policy (DOC-011)

---

## 1. Purpose

This note defines the labeling requirements that every Al Dente S.r.l. product must satisfy before it is shipped to a customer in another European Union member state. It exists to make sure that artwork, packaging copy and lot management are aligned with EU food information rules, so that goods are not blocked at destination and so that retail customers (including those governed by the GDO capitolato, DOC-014) receive compliant labels.

The objective is a single, consistent reference for everyone who designs, approves or produces export labels, reducing the risk of non-conforming artwork reaching the market and of avoidable returns under the returns and quality complaints policy.

---

## 2. Scope and applicability

This note applies to all finished pasta products in consumer and catering packaging that leave the Al Dente S.r.l. plant for an EU destination. It complements, and does not replace, the customer-specific requirements that may be set out in the GDO capitolato (DOC-014). Where a customer specification is stricter than this note, the stricter requirement prevails, provided it does not conflict with mandatory EU rules.

---

## 3. Labeling language

Each label must be written in the **destination market language**. The information presented to the final consumer has to be in a language that is easily understood in the country where the product is sold. A label prepared only in the language of origin is not sufficient for export; the destination market language is the governing requirement.

In practice this means that, for a given shipment, Export sales must confirm the destination country before artwork is finalised, and Packaging design must ensure that the consumer-facing mandatory information is provided in the destination market language. Multilingual labels are acceptable as long as the destination market language is present and legible.

---

## 4. Mandatory fields

Every export label must carry the following mandatory information. None of these fields may be omitted, and each must be legible on the consumer pack:

| # | Mandatory field | What it covers |
|---|-----------------|----------------|
| 1 | Ingredients | The list of ingredients, in descending order of quantity, as defined for the product |
| 2 | Allergens | The allergens present, emphasised within or alongside the ingredient information |
| 3 | Net weight | The net quantity of product in the pack |
| 4 | TMC | The date of minimum durability (termine minimo di conservazione) |
| 5 | Lot code | The lot code that identifies the production batch |
| 6 | EU operator address | The name and address of the EU food business operator responsible for the product |

The six fields above are checked together as a block during artwork approval. An artwork that is missing any one of ingredients, allergens, net weight, TMC, lot code or EU operator address is treated as non-conforming and cannot be released for print.

---

## 5. Lot code and traceability link

The **lot code** on the label is the bridge between the consumer pack and the internal production and quality records. It must match the lot identification used in production so that, in the event of a complaint or recall, the product on the market can be traced back through the HACCP plan (DOC-013) and the quality management system (DOC-012). Because the lot code is also the reference used when handling export complaints under the returns and quality complaints policy (DOC-011), it must be applied accurately and remain readable for the whole shelf life expressed by the TMC.

---

## 6. Responsibilities

- **Export sales:** confirms the destination country and therefore the destination market language before artwork is finalised; flags any customer-specific labeling clause from the GDO capitolato (DOC-014).
- **Packaging design:** prepares artwork carrying all six mandatory fields (ingredients, allergens, net weight, TMC, lot code, EU operator address) in the destination market language.
- **Quality Assurance:** approves the artwork against this note, verifies the EU operator address and the allergen information, and authorises release for print.
- **Production planning:** ensures the printed lot code matches the actual production batch so the traceability link holds.

---

This note is part of the Al Dente S.r.l. quality management system. Any deviation must be authorised by the Quality Assurance Department and recorded.
